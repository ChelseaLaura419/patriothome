FAQ

Is the Inspector for Patriot Home Inspections Plus Certified?

Yes, the inspector is a certified home inspector and member of the International Association of Certified Home Inspectors (InterNachi). InterNachi is one of the most reputable, world’s largest nonprofit associations of residential and commercial property inspectors. InterNachi inspectors are required to go through extensive training and a certification process program with annual continuing education requirements and periodic testing to maintain membership.

Is the Inspector for Patriot Home Inspections Plus Licensed?

Yes, the inspector has a current home inspection license with the State of Florida. License number HI10750.

Is the Inspector for Patriot Home Inspections Plus Certified?

Yes, as required by the State of Florida, the inspector maintains a Commercial General Liability insurance policy.

How long does a full home inspection take?

A full home inspection typically depends on the size, age and condition of your home. An average inspection can take 2-4 hours. Additional time may be required if ancillary services are added to the standard full inspection.

When can I expect to receive my report?

Reports will be sent within 24 hours to the client who has signed the inspection agreement. If you would like the report sent to anyone other than who signed the agreement, it must be requested in writing. The inspection report will cover any deficiencies or concerns about the home’s pertinent components and systems as described within the InterNachi Standard of Practice. Pictures will be included to document deficiencies. Recommendations will be made to address these deficiencies.

Do I need to attend the inspection?

Although it is not necessary, we encourage and prefer that clients be present for inspections to observe and ask questions. The inspector will always make time to discuss the final report. In the case of a wind mitigation inspection, it is required that an adult at least 18 years of age be present.

Can I ask questions during the inspection?

Absolutely! You may ask questions as the inspection is in progress or once the inspection is complete. The inspector has a lot to evaluate and most inspectors have a routine so that they do not overlook anything, so it is suggested to wait until the end of the inspection. Once the inspection is complete your inspector will summarize his findings. Questions can also be asked and answered once you review the final report.

How can I prepare for my inspection?

There are several things you could do to help with the inspection process including:

  • Have documentation of any updates and/or improvement performed on the house.
  • The electrical, water and HVAC systems should be turned on prior to inspection.
  • Your inspector is conducting a visual inspection, so access is important. Be sure to allow for total access, especially to key systems such as electrical panels, heating and cooling systems and attic spaces.
  • If you have a pet please let the inspector know. Dogs should be secured in an area isolated from the inspection areas.

How do I make payment?

Payments are made to Patriot Home Inspections Plus prior to or at the time of your inspection. Acceptable forms of payment include Check, Cash, Money order and/or Zelle/Quick Pay.
