Full Home Inspections

A full home inspection is a non-invasive visual examination of the accessible areas and specific systems of a residential property. Inspections are conducted following the Standard Operating Procedures set forth by the International Association of Certified Home Inspectors. Systems inspected include, but are not limited to, the following:

  • Roof
  • Exterior (Cladding, structure, deck, patio, porch)
  • Attic, Insulation and Ventilation
  • Electrical & Plumbing
  • Heating & Cooling
  • Interior Structure (Including doors, windows)
  • Built in Appliances
  • Fireplace and Wood Stoves
  • Attached garages

4-Point Inspections

Prior to the purchase of your home or during the renewal of an insurance policy, insurance carriers may require a 4-point inspection if your home is 25 years old or more. The purpose of the inspection is to help the insurance company determine your home’s eligibility for insurance.

A 4-point inspection is a concentrated home inspection that asses the existing condition and any improvements that have been made, to the roof, heating and cooling, plumbing and electrical systems. Should any major deficiencies be identified in the inspection, the report will give you the opportunity to discuss needed improvements with your agent prior to submitting your insurance application. Patriot Home Inspections Plus will conduct a thorough 4-point inspection, completing a report outlining the details of your home for your insurance agent.

Wind Mitigation

According to the Florida State Statue 627.711 insurance carriers are responsible to clearly notify you of the availability of premium discounts or credits based on your home’s construction techniques and improvements made to reduce the amount of loss in a windstorm.

To take advantage of these discounts a Wind Mitigation Inspection will be required by your insurance carrier. Documentation is completed by your qualified licensed inspector on a specific form created by the Florida Office of Insurance Regulation OIR-B1-1802. Systems and components inspected include:

  • Doors and Windows
  • Storm Shutters
  • Roof materials and coverings
  • Roof and walls attachments
  • Roof shape

The inspection report will include feedback regarding the wind resistant features your home already possess and indicates the improvements that can be made to further reduce the risk to your home from wind. Once your insurance agent receives the final report it will help your agent in qualify your home for potential discounts or credits.
